This is an unordered list. It uses bullet point by default.

  • flour
  • milk
  • eggs

This is an ordered list. It uses numbered items

  1. flour
  2. eggs
  3. milk

This list has a value added inside its' list tag in order to change the position.

  1. Head north on N Halsted St
  2. Turn right on W Diversey Pkwy
  3. Turn left on N Orchard St

If you add 'reversed' inside the ordered list tag you will get:

  1. one
  2. two
  3. three

This shows how to make a defined list in HTML. Shamelessly copied from the internet.


      <dt>study</dt>
      <dd>The devotion of time and attention to acquiring knowledge on an academic subject, especially by
      means of books</dd>
      <dt>design</dt>
      <dd>A plan or drawing produced to show the look and function or workings of a building, garment, or
      other object before it is built or made</dd>
      <dd>Purpose, planning, or intention that exists or is thought to exist behind an action, fact, or
      material object</dd>
      <dt>business</dt>
      <dt>work</dt>
      <dd>A person's regular occupation, profession, or trade</dd>
      </dl>

This shows how to nest items within the list to make it look more palatable. Practice will be needed in order to make it correctly.


      <ol>
      <li>Walk the dog</li>
      <liFold laundry/li>
      <li> Go to the grocery and buy:
      <ul>
      <liMilk/li>
      <liBread/li>
      <liCheese/li>
      </ul>
      </li>
      <liMow the lawn/li>
      <liMake dinner/li>
      </ol>

The result:

  1. Walk the dog
  2. Fold laundry
  3. Go to the grocery and buy:
    • Milk
    • Bread
    • Cheese
  4. Mow the lawn
  5. Make dinner
